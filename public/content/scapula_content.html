Scapula

The scapula, or shoulder blade, is a large, flat, triangular bone located on the posterior aspect of the ribcage. It connects the humerus (upper arm bone) with the clavicle and plays a key role in shoulder movement and stability.

Key Features

  • Spine of Scapula: A prominent ridge that separates the supraspinous and infraspinous fossae
  • Acromion: Forms the highest point of the shoulder and connects with the clavicle
  • Glenoid Cavity: A shallow socket that articulates with the head of the humerus to form the shoulder joint
  • Coracoid Process: A bony projection serving as a muscle attachment point

Muscle Attachments

The scapula provides attachment sites for numerous muscles that support shoulder motion and posture, including:

  • Supraspinatus
  • Infraspinatus
  • Subscapularis
  • Teres major and minor
  • Trapezius and deltoid

Clinical Relevance

Scapular dyskinesis, or abnormal movement of the scapula, can contribute to shoulder pain and reduced performance in athletes. Proper scapular positioning and strength are critical in shoulder rehabilitation.
